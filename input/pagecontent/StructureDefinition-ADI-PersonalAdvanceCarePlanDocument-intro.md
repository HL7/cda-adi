A Personal Advance Care Plan (ACP) document is a kind of advance healthcare directive document which is authored by the subject of the document (the person or patient). The person (or their representative) is required to sign the Personal ACP document and is represented as the personal attester.

If other care team members are involved in the creation of an advance healthcare directive document, they may be included as “facilitators” or contributors to the process, and they may or may not be attesters (signers) of the resultant document, but they are never considered the author of the content.  Others who sign the document are represented as attesters, with their role of witness (mode=official) or notary (mode=professional).

A Personal ACP Composition which is being exchanged or made accessible always includes a representation of the completed original document, called the "source form". All types of advance healthcare directive documents always must contain the source form representation in the Source Form section. 

A Personal ACP Composition may include other textual information organized in standard structural sections, which may or may not include machine processable encoded entries.

Additional structured information that may be represented includes:

* The person’s appointed healthcare agent(s), 
* Treatment intervention **_and_** care experience preferences, with the ability to further specify those preferences under potential, future health scenarios
* Administrative information; Completion information; Additional Documentation; and Upon death preferences; and 
* Witness and notary information (if that information is required for the specific form being represented to be considered valid.) 

An advance healthcare directive document must always contain the source form representation in the Source Form section. It may include other textual information organized in standard structural sections, which may or may not include machine processable encoded entries.
