A Portable Medical Order (PMO) document is a kind of advance healthcare directive document which is authored by a sufficiently qualified provider. Each state determines the provider qualifications required to author a PMO. The provider is required to sign the PMO document and is represented in the document as the legal attester.

The person/patient (or their representative) also is required to sign this type of PMO document and is represented as a personal attester.

If other care team members are involved in the creation of these types of documents, they may be included as facilitators or contributors to the process, and they may or may not be signers of the resultant document. Other care team members who sign the document are represented as professional attesters.

A PMO document which is being exchanged or made accessible always includes a representation of the completed original document, called the "source form". All types of advance healthcare directive documents always must contain the source form representation in the Source Form section.  

Additional structured information that may be represented includes:

* Medical orders for certain treatment interventions intended to be honored and followed by other care providers
* The person’s appointed healthcare agent,
* Administrative information; Completion information; Additional documentation; Upon death preferences, especially organ donation, and
* Witness and notary information (if that level of attestation is required for the represented form to be considered valid).
